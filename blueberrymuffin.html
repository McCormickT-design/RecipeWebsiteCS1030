Blueberry Muffins

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ Cups (320g) All Purpose flour
  • 1 Cup (200g) Granulated sugar
  • ¼ Cup (50g)Light brown sugar
  • 3 tsp Baking Powder
  • 1 tsp Cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp Salt
  • 2 Cups (280g) Fresh blueberries, plus more for sprinkling on top
  • ¼ Cup (55g) Melted Butter (unsalted)
  • ¼ Cup (50g) Nuetral oil such as canola or vegetable
  • 2 Large eggs, room temp
  • ½ Cup (120g) Coconut milk
  • ½ Cup (118g) Milk, room temp
  • 2 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • ¼ tsp Almond Extract
  • Raw sugar, for sprinkling on top

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425F and line a cupcake tin with 11 paper liners (I use small squares of parchment paper and mold them to the cupcake tin with a glass or cup that fits in the tin).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, brown sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. Then, add in the blueberries and mix to distribute
  3. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the butter, oil, eggs, coconut milk, milk, vanilla, and almond extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and use a rubber spatula to stir the two together
  5. Evenly distribute the batter between the 11 liners (I use an ice cream scoop to get make this easier). They should fill each liner.
  6. Sprinkle the tops with the extra blueberries and raw sugar.
  7. Bake for 20-23 minutes or until the edges a golden and the blueberries have burst
  8. Allow muffins to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes.
  9. Transfer muffins to a cooling rack to continue cooling. Store in an airtight container on your counter for a couple days or in the fridge for about a week
Recipe adapted from Jenna Barnard @ butternutbakery
